Sarson Ka Saag

About Sarson Ka Saag

Sarson ka Saag is a traditional North Indian winter dish made with mustard greens, spinach, and spices. This nutritious and delicious green curry is typically served with Makki di Roti (cornmeal flatbread) and a dollop of butter or ghee on top.

Ingredients

  • 500g mustard greens (sarson)
  • 200g spinach (palak)
  • 100g fenugreek leaves (methi)
  • 2 tbsp maize flour (makki ka atta)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter

Instructions

  1. Clean, wash, and chop the mustard greens, spinach, and fenugreek leaves.
  2. In a large pot, add the greens with a little water and cook until soft.
  3. Drain the greens and blend them into a coarse paste.
  4. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp of ghee. Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© until golden brown.
  5. Add the blended greens to the pan and mix well.
  6. Sprinkle maize flour and stir continuously to mix it evenly.
  7. Add salt to taste and cook for 10-15 minutes until the saag is thick and flavorful.
  8. Serve hot with Makki di Roti and a dollop of butter or ghee.
