Siddu Recipe

Traditional Himachali Siddu

Siddu is a steamed, stuffed bread from the Himachal Pradesh region in India, traditionally made with wheat flour and filled with savory spiced fillings. Enjoy this delightful dish with ghee, dal, or chutney for an authentic taste of Himachali cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups wheat flour
  • 1 tsp dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green chilies
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • Ghee for serving

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix yeast and sugar with warm water. Let it rest for 10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine wheat flour and the yeast mixture. Knead into a soft dough, adding water as needed. Cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  3. For the filling, combine mashed potatoes, chopped onions, green chilies, salt, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Mix well.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll each one out into a circle. Place a spoonful of filling in the center, fold over, and seal the edges.
  5. Steam the filled dough balls for 15-20 minutes until they are cooked through.
  6. Serve warm with ghee, dal, or chutney.
